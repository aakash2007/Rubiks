Following are the guidelines from Guinness World Records. Please go through instructions and guidelines very carefully and make sure you understand every point. Violation of any of these would lead to direct disqualification of the complete attempt.

INSTRUCTIONS AND GUIDELINES
  • The Rubik's cubes need to be checked and scrambled to 40 random moves by the officials, well before the attempt.The officials will hand the cubes to all the participants on spot.

  • If more than 10% of total participants are not able to successfully solve, the record will become void even if current record is beaten.

THE CUBES WILL BE COLLECTED FROM YOUR ROOMS TONIGHT. 

Today is the last day to practice. 

Please ensure that you submit your cube tonight to our volunteers during room to room collection. (9:00 p.m. - 11:59 p.m.)
  • Every person has to attempt the record in the same 30 minutes window.
  • The participant can leave anytime after solving the scrambled cube.
  • Any form of written or printed aid is strictly against the spirit of the record. DO NOT carry any solution guide, printed algorithm or any form of aid to the event. 
  • Do Not touch anyone else's cube during the official attempt. You might advise but cannot provide physical or visual aid to a fellow participant.
  • The whole event will be recorded. Recordings and photographic evidence will be sent to Guinness World Records for verification.

  • Bus facility has been arranged to take students from their hostels to the Glider's club ground.
  • The OOP test has been shifted to avoid any clash. Special Bus has been arranged to take participants directly from IPC to the event site.
  • If the attempt is successful, every participant can get official Guinness certificate.
